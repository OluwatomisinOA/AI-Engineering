The right choice depends on your model type, interpretability need, and domain considerations.

1. Drop Redundant Features
- Remove one feature from each highly correlated pair, preferably keeping the one with stronger target correlation or better interpretability.

2. Combine Features
- Create composite features through averaging, weighted sums, or domain-infirmed combinations. This preserve information while  reducing dimensionality.

3. Apply Dimensionality Reduction
- Techniques like PCA transform correlated features into uncorrelated principal components, though at the cost interpretability.

4. Let the Model Handle It
- Tree-based models and neural networks can work with correlated features.
