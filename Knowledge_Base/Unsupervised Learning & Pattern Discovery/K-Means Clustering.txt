It is designed to discover hidden patterns in unlabeled data. It automatically groups data points into K distinct clusters based on their similarity, using distance as the primary measure.


What Problem Does K-Means Solve?

K-Means tackles the fundamental question: How can we automatically discover natural groupings in this data?

The algorithm identifies structure by ensuring that data points within the same cluster share high similarity, while points in different clusters remian distinctly separate.


The Core Idea of K-Means

K-Means seprates on a principle: represent each cluster by a central point called centroid, then assign every data point to its nearest centroid.

01 Choose K
- Decide how many clusters you want to find
02 Initialize Centroids
- Place K representative points in the data space
03 Assign Points
- Group each data point with its closest centroid
04 Update Centers
- Recalculate centroids based on current assignments


Choosing the Value of K:

- Elbow Method
Plot within-cluster sum of squares (inertia) for different K values. Look for the "elbow" where adding clusters yields diminishing returns.
- Silhouette Score
Measures how similar points are to their own clusters versus other clusters. SCores range from -1 to 1, with higher values indicating better-defined clusters.
- Domain Knowledge
Sometimes business context dictates K-if you're segmenting into premium, standard7, and budget tiers, K=3 is predetermined by strategy.


When K-Means Fails

- Initialization Sensitivity
Random centroid placement can lead to different results each run. Poor initial positions may trap the algorithm in suboptimal local minima.
Solution: Use K-Means++ initialization or run multiple times
- Spherical Assumption
K-Means struggles with non-spherical. elongated, or irregularly-shaped clusters. It forces circular boundaries that may not match your data's true structure. Better option: DBSCAN for arbitrary shapes.
- Outlier Sensitivity
Extreme values dramatically pull centroids away from cluster centers, distorting results. A single outlier can corrupt and entire cluster's position.
Preprocessing: Remove or cap outliers first.
- Pre-specified K
Requiring K upfront is both a feature and a limitation-you must estimate the number of clusters before analysis, which isn't possible in exploratory work.
