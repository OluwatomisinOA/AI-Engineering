Histograms provide a fundamental view into how your data values are distributed across their range. By grouping continuous data into bins and counting frequencies, histograms reveal shape and characteristics of your variable's distribution.

1. Identify Skewness
- Quickly spot whether your data is symmetric, left-skewed, or right-skewed, which influences feature transformation decisions.

2. Detect Multi-Modal Data
- Discover if your data contains multiple peaks, suggesting the presence of distinct subgroups or populations within your dataset.

3. Spot outliers
- Identify extreme values that appear as isolated bars far from the main distribution, whihc may require special handling.

4. Validate ML Assumptions
- Many algorithms assumed normally distributed features. Histograms help you verify these assumptions before modeling.

Code Example:

plt.hist(df["salary"], bins=20)
plt.xlabel("Salary")
plt.ylabel("Frequency")
plt.title("Salary Distribution")
plt.show()


Histograms — all numeric columns at once:
