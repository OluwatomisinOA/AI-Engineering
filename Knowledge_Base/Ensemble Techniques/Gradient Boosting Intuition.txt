Gradient boosting represents a powerful ensemble technique that has revolutionized machine learning. 
It build models sequentially, where each new model focuses specifically on correcting the errors made by its predecessors.


Parallel vs Sequential Learning:

Bagging(Random Forest):
- Predictions are averaged together
- Errors cancel out through averaging
- Reduces variance in predcitions

Boosting(Gradient Boosting)
- Predictions are added together
- Errors are directly corrected
- Reduces bias in prediction

Key Insight: Bagging reduces variance by averaging independent predictions, while boosting reduces bias by explicitly learning from previous mistakes.
Boosting doesn't fit the raw target values repeatedly. Instead, it fits the errors made by previous models, creating a progress in the refinement process
